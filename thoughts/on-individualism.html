on individualism.

I feel like I am poisoned by the idea of being individualistic. Yes, it’s important to be strong as an individual, but the notion that we must be completely self-reliant feels like a consumerist lie. We are utterly dependent on others. It’s strange how hard we try to assert individuality in a society that, by definition, is built on collaboration. We cannot even eat without the work of others, yet we act as if we don’t need anyone...

This extends to almost every part of our lives, from our basic needs like food and healthcare to our possessions. We need society. We need each other, physically, emotionally, and practically.

Individualism is a capitalist lie. It convinces us that we are stronger alone while making us consume more and more. We all want our own place to live, our own car to drive, our own bicycle to ride, always more own things, never enough. We consume endlessly in the name of self-sufficiency, buying books we don’t read, throwing away food we don’t eat, discarding clothes we never wear. This mindset inevitably drives us to solitude, where we try to prove our self-sufficiency. But at what cost?

We are all human beings, facing more or less the same struggles. Yet, we deal with them alone. We have been conditioned to believe that showing vulnerability is weakness. We cry alone at home, we keep our problems to ourselves, and we refuse to ask for help. We are all “self-sufficient” individuals, yet we are lonelier than ever. But why? If we share the same struggles, why can’t we have the patience, time, and energy to understand each other?

So individualism is a consumerist lie that dictates us to be alone in all ways. At the end of the road, we become self-sufficient individuals, we retreat into our own mental bubbles and we forget how to respect each other when our lives intersect. We forget how to share feelings, how to talk about emotions, how to truly connect. And the more isolated we become, the more we cling to this illusion of independence.

This is the price we pay for individualism. It seems to me like a brainwashed mindset to believe we need to be individual at all costs.
